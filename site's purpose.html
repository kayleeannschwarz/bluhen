the beginning of blühen

when presented with my capstone expectations, i wanted to code a site for a topic that i’m passionate about. with that, bluhen was born. this passion project born out of my love for low-toxic living has been so! fun! to create. i’ve been able to channel myself and who i am into this project while also practicing my coding, branding, writing and creative skills.

i’m a firm believer in non-toxic and intentional living and wanted to condense the information i’ve learned over the past years into one place. the idea of filling our homes with better and cleaner products is becoming more popular, and i wanted to capitalize on that momentum and the target audience it brings.





the branding of blühen

when dreaming of what i wanted this site to be, i wrote out a list of words that represented what non-toxic living meant to me. the more i reflected on it, the more i thought about why i chose to live non-toxic. i wanted better, to be healthier, be less dependent on western medicine and corporations, fill my space with intentionality. i wanted to flourish. i chose to tie in the idea of flourish with my roots, a nod to the german concoctions my dad would whip up when we were sick as kids. with some quick translation help from my dad, the name bluhen was chosen.

in order to enforce what i’ve learned about user experience, i knew i needed to curate my branding to my target audience. i chose fonts that were fresh and quirky and a neutral color palette to capitalize on the current neutral palette trend. i wanted the site to feel earthy, give off an air of ease and formulate a sense of flourishing. each decision i made in regard to the design of the site was based on tangible research.
